2x2 Rubiks Cube

The 2x2 is a great starting point for beginners. It has no edge or center pieces—just 8 corner pieces to solve. By learning a few simple algorithms and practicing layer by layer, you’ll quickly build your cubing skills and prepare for bigger challenges like the 3x3!

Start Learning 2x2

3x3 Rubiks Cube

The 3x3 is the classic puzzle that started it all. With 6 faces, 9 stickers per side, and over 43 quintillion combinations, it may look impossible—but it’s fully solvable with logic and practice! Learn the layer-by-layer (LBL) method, understand core cube mechanics, and start mastering algorithms to solve it efficiently.

Start Learning 3x3

4x4 Rubiks Cube

The 4x4 adds a new level of complexity to the classic cube. With no fixed centers and extra edge pieces, you'll learn new techniques like center pairing and edge matching before solving it like a 3x3. It’s a fun challenge that sharpens your skills and introduces you to advanced cubing concepts!

Start Learning 4x4

Pyraminx

The Pyraminx is a pyramid-shaped puzzle with 4 faces and rotating tips. It’s beginner-friendly and easier than it looks! Start by solving the tips and centers, then use simple moves to align the edges. A great intro to twisty puzzles and a fun way to boost your confidence in speedsolving!

Start Learning Pyraminx
